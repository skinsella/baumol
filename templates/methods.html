Methods and data

This page documents every series, every transformation, and every methodological choice the site relies on. The intent is full reproducibility from public sources without authentication.

Sample period

Quarterly labour cost data are reported from 2008Q1 onwards. The CSO predecessor series under NACE Rev. 1 stops at end-2007 and does not splice cleanly into EHECS without sector-level concordance work, so this site uses 2008Q1–latest as the headline window. Where annual Eurostat data extend back further (LCI from 2000), they are used as a robustness check and noted explicitly.

Sector taxonomy

NACE Rev. 2 letters A21 throughout. The Baumol stagnant/progressive split used on the headline page is a prior, not a result of the analysis:

  • Stagnant prior: H (Transport & storage), I (Accommodation & food), P (Education), Q (Human health & social work), R (Arts), S (Other services).
  • Progressive prior: C (Manufacturing), J (Information & communication), K (Financial & insurance).

The regressions on the productivity page do not depend on the prior — the stagnant/progressive split is only used in the share-shift descriptive chart.

MNC distortion

Sectors C and J in Ireland are heavily affected by multinational activity: contract manufacturing, IP onshoring, aircraft leasing, and intra-group royalty flows can drive headline GVA and produce labour-productivity numbers that are not informative about the domestic economy. Where productivity is used (the regression on the productivity page), the headline specification excludes C and J as a robustness check; the modified GNI* / domestic-only adjustment from CSO is the next planned refinement.

Compensation aggregate construction

The aggregate compensation series displayed on the labour-costs page is constructed as: hourly labour cost × paid hours per week × employment × 52, summed across sectors. This understates total compensation of employees in the National Accounts by a small margin because EHECS excludes some segments (very small enterprises in some NACE codes; agriculture). It is used here as a within-CSO-EHECS aggregate where the wagebill is consistent with the hourly labour-cost series being plotted, not as a substitute for COE.

Failure-premium claim adjudication

Each claim on the failure-premium page is tested against named, public sources, linked from the claim card. The standard for "refuted" is that the public data materially contradict the claim as stated; "reframed" is reserved for claims whose numbers are correct but whose inferred mechanism is misspecified (typically: comparing a demand-side subsidy line to a capital-programme line, or making an international comparison without like-for-like inclusions). Verdicts can move on later refreshes and the site notes the date of each.
