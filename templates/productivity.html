Productivity and pay

The Baumol mechanism predicts that low-productivity-growth sectors see faster labour-cost growth than their own productivity warrants. Two tests on Irish data: β-convergence in hourly labour cost, and the cost-growth–productivity-gap regression.

What's missing here

This page intentionally stops short of a full ULC decomposition because the sectoral productivity series for Ireland are heavily distorted by MNC activity in C (Manufacturing) and J (ICT). Eurostat nama_10_a64 records the gross GVA those sectors generate domestically, but the implied labour productivity is not a domestic-economy number. The methods page documents the modified-GVA adjustment used for cross-checks; updates planned in the next refresh.
