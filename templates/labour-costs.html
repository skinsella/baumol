Labour cost breakdown by sector

What does an hour of labour cost a firm in each part of the Irish economy, and how has that changed? The pictures below split the cost into wages and non-wage components and show the level by sector over time.

What is "hourly labour cost"?

Hourly labour cost is what a firm actually pays out per hour of work — wages and salaries plus employer social-insurance contributions, training, and other non-wage labour costs. It is the most directly Baumol-relevant series because Baumol's prediction is about the cost of labour relative to its productivity.

Source: CSO EHQ03 (Earnings, Hours and Employment Costs Survey), {{ data_period }}. NACE A (agriculture) sits outside this survey.
